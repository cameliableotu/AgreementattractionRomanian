Purpose of Study

In this research, Adina Camelia Bleotu, of the University of Bucharest, and Brian Dillon, of the University of Massachusetts Amherst, are investigating the processes involved in the comprehension of English sentences. The information collected in this study will be used in the preparation of papers for presentation in international conferences and journals on language. You are eligible to participate in this study because you are a native speaker of Romanian, over 18 years of age.

Procedures

In this study, you will first be asked a few questions about your language background, and then you will be asked to fill in a sentence by choosing the best answer out of two answers as fast as you can. Given that you are given only a few seconds to make your choice, you will have to be very fast in giving your answer. The entire process should take about 15 minutes, if you work efficiently. Upon completion, you will be rewarded with course credit, provided that your responses indicate that you did read the dialogs and questions carefully.

Risks

There are no known risks involved in this experimental procedure. However, with any online research there is a slight risk of a breach of confidentiality. To guard against this possibility, we will not collect any uniquely identifying information (such as your MTurk worker ID) on the servers where our data is collected.

Benefits

You will receive no immediate personal benefits from your participation in this research other than monetary compensation.

Confidentiality

Your results will be kept confidential to the best of our ability. Your name will not appear in any publication or presentation. To maximize confidentiality, your results will be assigned to a subject number even for our own analyses, and information linking you to your data will be kept confidential.

Refusal or Withdrawal of Participation

Your participation in this study is purely voluntary. You do not have to provide any information that you are not comfortable providing. You may withdraw from this study at any time, though note that doing so means that you agree to not receive course credit.
